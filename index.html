A good travel card is more than “points”. It’s foreign exchange fees, airport lounges, bundled insurance and how your card behaves when something goes wrong abroad.

Travel card checklist

  • Foreign transaction fee 0% vs 1.75–2.5%
  • Lounge access Priority Pass, Plaza, own lounges
  • Travel insurance medical, delay, cancellation
  • FX markup in app live exchange rate vs hidden spread
  • Support abroad 24/7 help when card is blocked

This site is independent and informational. It does not issue cards.

1. Foreign transaction fees (FX)

Many cards still charge a hidden tax on every purchase abroad – often between 1.75% and 2.5% on top of the exchange rate. On a long trip, this quietly adds up.

  • 0% FX cards are best if you travel often.
  • Check the issuer’s own FX table – don’t just trust “no foreign fee” marketing.
  • Some cards waive fees only in specific regions or currencies.

2. Lounge access & airport experience

Lounge access can easily be worth more than the annual fee if you travel a lot. But not all lounge programs are equal.

  • Check how many visits per year are included.
  • Look at guest access for family or partner.
  • See if the card is tied to a single airline or an open network.
Filter for lounge access cards on Choose.Creditcard →

3. Travel insurance bundled with your card

Many cards advertise “travel insurance”, but the actual coverage varies a lot.

  • Is medical treatment abroad covered – and up to which limit?
  • Are delays and cancellations included, or just lost luggage?
  • Do you need to pay the trip with the card to activate the insurance?

Always read the full policy document. Use card comparison tools as a starting point, not as legal advice.
