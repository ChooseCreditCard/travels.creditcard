When is a dedicated travel card worth it?

If you travel a few times per year, the “hidden taxes” on your existing card can easily outweigh any annual fee on a well-designed travel card.

  • You pay > 1.75–2.5% FX markup on every purchase abroad.
  • You don’t have medical coverage or trip cancellation in your card.
  • You regularly buy airport lounge access separately.
  • Your card is often blocked when travelling – and support is slow.

What to compare in a travel credit card

1. FX fees

Many cards still add a 1.75–2.5% fee on top of the exchange rate. On a long trip, that’s a quiet tax on every restaurant, hotel and taxi ride. Prefer 0% FX or close.

2. Airport lounges

Lounge access can easily be worth more than the annual fee if you travel often. Check visit limits, guest policy, and which airports are actually covered.

3. Travel insurance

“Includes travel insurance” can mean almost anything. Look for medical coverage limits, delays & cancellations, and whether you must pay the trip with the card to activate it.

4. Support abroad

When your card is blocked in a foreign country, you need fast human help. 24/7 support, app chat, and clear emergency numbers are underrated features.

5. Rewards & points

Points are the extra, not the core. First fix FX, lounges and insurance. Then look at earn rates, partner programs, and how easy it is to actually redeem your rewards.
